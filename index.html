Field Season 2026 · vol. I

A collective notebook for
the station's hummingbirds.

Every observation a member of the station logs here drops a pin on our shared map. The idea is simple: see a bird, record it, and help us build a picture of how the flock moves through Gothic this summer.

How it works

Spot a hummingbird — at a feeder, on a flower, banded, unbanded, anywhere on station. Open Submit Sighting, fill in what you saw, snap a photo if you can, and submit. Your pin appears on the Live Map.

· · ·

What to record

The form asks for species, sex, whether the bird has a color mark, GPS coordinates, time, and photos of the bird and any flower it was visiting. All fields except species are optional — log what you have, skip what you don't.

Why it matters

The 2025–2026 winter brought unusually low snowpack to the East River valley, and we expect flowering phenology to shift early. Distributed sightings from the full station community give us temporal and spatial resolution no single observer can match.
